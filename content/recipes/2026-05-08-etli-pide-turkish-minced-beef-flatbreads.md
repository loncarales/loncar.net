## Instructions

### Make the dough

1. In a large bowl, whisk together the milk, water, yeast, sugar, and yogurt until the yeast dissolves.
2. Add the flour, olive oil, salt, egg white, and baking powder.
3. Knead for a few minutes until you get a smooth, pliable dough.
4. Divide the dough into 8 equal pieces.
5. Shape each piece into a tight ball on a lightly floured surface.
6. Cover with a clean tea towel and let the dough rest.

### Make the filling

1. Finely chop the onions, red pointed pepper, green pointed pepper, and tomatoes.
2. Place the chopped vegetables into a fine-mesh sieve over a bowl.
3. Press gently with a spoon to remove excess moisture.
4. Heat 30 g olive oil in a skillet over medium-high heat.
5. Add the Biber Salçası and minced meat.
6. Break the meat up with a wooden spoon and cook until browned.
7. Season with salt, black pepper, hot paprika, and dried thyme.
8. Remove from the heat and stir in the drained onion, pepper, and tomato mixture.

### Shape the pide

1. Preheat the oven to 220 °C.
2. Line two baking sheets with parchment paper.
3. On a floured surface, roll each dough ball into an oblong rectangle, roughly 25 x 15 cm.
4. Place 4 dough pieces onto each prepared baking sheet, leaving space between them.
5. Spoon about 2 tablespoons of filling into the center of each piece.
6. Spread the filling out while leaving a clear 1.5 cm border around the edges.
7. Fold the long edges slightly inward over the filling.
8. Pinch and twist the top and bottom ends firmly to create the classic boat shape.

### Glaze and bake

1. In a small bowl, whisk together the reserved egg yolk, 1 tablespoon yogurt, and nigella seeds.
2. Brush the mixture over the folded dough borders.
3. Bake one tray at a time at 220 °C for about 20 minutes, until the edges are puffed and golden brown.
4. Garnish with chopped spring onions if you like.
5. Serve warm.

The secret to a good pide is draining the vegetable mixture before adding it to the meat. This keeps the topping rich and flavorful without making the soft dough soggy during baking.

The yogurt and olive oil in the dough help the crust bake up golden while staying pillowy inside.

You can make this with minced beef or lamb. Lamb gives it a stronger, more traditional flavor, while beef keeps it a little milder.
