## Instructions

1. Carefully slice a pocket into the side of each chicken breast, making sure not to cut all the way through.
2. Stuff each pocket with a generous slice of mozzarella and a piece of sun-dried tomatoes.
3. Secure the opening with toothpicks so the filling stays inside while cooking.
4. Rub the chicken with olive oil and season it generously with oregano, basil, red pepper flakes, salt, and pepper.
5. Heat a pan over medium-high heat and sear the chicken for about 3–4 minutes per side, until golden brown. The chicken does not need to be cooked through yet; this is just for flavor.
6. While the chicken sears, line a baking dish with parchment paper.
7. Arrange the zucchini planks in the dish to make a base.
8. Sprinkle the zucchini with Parmesan cheese.
9. Place the seared chicken breasts on top of the zucchini.
10. Add another layer of Parmesan on top of the chicken.
11. Bake at 200 °C for about 15–20 minutes, until the mozzarella is melted and the Parmesan is golden and bubbling.
12. Remove the toothpicks before serving.

## Notes

If your sun-dried tomatoes come in oil, use that oil for searing the chicken. It adds a deeper tomato flavor and makes the whole dish even better.

The zucchini and Parmesan on the bottom turn into a kind of savory crust, especially when the chicken juices and oil mingle with the cheese. It is a very satisfying example of what happens when you open the fridge, trust your instincts, and cook anyway.
