## Instructions

### Prepare the moisture mixture

1. In a medium bowl, whisk together the sour cream, eggs, olive oil, and a pinch of salt until smooth and pourable.
2. This mixture keeps the phyllo elastic and tender on the inside while it bakes.

### Prepare the pan and cheese

1. Generously butter the inside of a cast iron skillet or enameled baking pan.
2. Do not be shy with the butter, this helps create the crispy golden bottom crust.
3. Place the cheese in a bowl and gently crumble it with a fork.
4. Keep it in small, uneven pieces rather than turning it into a smooth paste, so the pita has little pockets of cheese throughout.

### Layer, fill, and roll

1. Lay one sheet of phyllo dough on a clean wooden board or work surface.
2. Spoon 2–3 tablespoons of the sour cream mixture over the sheet and spread it gently.
3. Scatter a light handful of crumbled cheese along the bottom edge of the sheet.
4. Do not overfill, less is more here.
5. Roll the sheet loosely into a long tube.
6. Place the tube along the outer edge of the buttered skillet, starting to form a spiral.
7. Continue filling, rolling, and coiling the phyllo tubes into the pan, working from the outside inward, until the skillet is filled.

### Glaze and bake

1. Preheat the oven to 220 °C.
2. In a small saucepan, gently heat 5 tablespoons of water with 20–25 g butter until the butter melts.
3. Just before baking, brush or sprinkle this hot buttered water evenly over the top of the raw spiral.
4. Bake for about 30 minutes, until the top is deep golden brown.
5. A little darker color on the ridges is fine, that is where the crunch lives.

### Rest and serve

1. Remove the sirnica from the oven and let it rest in the skillet for a few minutes.
2. Place a large cutting board over the skillet and carefully flip it over.
3. The sirnica should slide out, revealing the golden bottom crust.
4. Let it rest for a few more minutes before slicing.
5. Serve warm.

## Notes

The secret to a crispy-yet-tender sirnica is moisture. Each sheet should be gently coated so the pastry layers steam and puff as they bake, instead of drying out.

I used Travnički sir here, but you can also use good-quality Greek feta or Turkish beyaz peynir.

Using a cast iron skillet gives the sirnica a beautiful golden crust on the bottom, while the inside stays tender and savory.
